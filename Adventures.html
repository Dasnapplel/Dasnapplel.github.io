Cycling

Me, my dad, and my brother are all Dutch. Perhaps it's in our blood to cycle. Here are the major adventures that I've done on the bike.

Grand Rapids to Mackinaw City
(August 4-12, 2018)

My dad has always been a cycler and adventurer. In his past, he's cycled around all of South America, and on a Motorbike he Traveled from the Netherlands to Australia. I guess he wanted to let me experience a taste of that same type of adventure, so at some point, we decided to cycle across all of Michigan together. We planned on heading to Mackinaw City, because then we could head to Mackinaw Island and have a sort of victory lap around the island! In the end, this trip took five days, and we traveled around 240 miles total. I was 12 years old.

Grand Rapids to Grand Traverse Lighthouse
(August 14-19, 2022)

After a few years, my brother became 14 and he and my dad both wanted to do a cycling trip together as well. My brother decided to set the destination as Great Wolf Lodge for fun, and they set off. I wanted to have fun too though, so in the end, my dad decided to make two trips, one with my brother to Traverse City, and one with me. I decided to set my destination as the Grand Traverse Lighthouse, because it was located at what could be looked at as the 'pinky' of Michigan. This way I could say that I cycled to the 'middle' and 'pinky' fingers of the state. In the end, this trip took four days, and we traveled around 180 miles. I was 16 years old.

Lowell to Pewamo
(July 24, 2022)

To do long distance cycling trips, some training and preperation is definetely required. To practice experiencing a day of cycling, my dad and I decided to cycle from Lowell county to Pewamo county. We took the Fred Meijer Grand River Valley Trail, and traveled about 50 miles. We also saw a cool museum about the trains that used to travel on the trail a long time ago!

Road Trips

Throughout the years, I've been on some really fun adventures by car. Here are a few of them.

Mall of America
(May 22-24, 2024)

After graduating highschool, me and two other friends decided to do a road trip together for fun. Since elementery school, there's been a place that's been nearly foreshadowed as a vacation spot that we would take, and that was the Mall of America. Since we were all somewhat introverted nerds, we joked that we postponed the stereotypical teenager-mall-hang-out to the end, and that we prioritized quality of quantity. It was fun!

Michigan Tech
(January 11-16, 2024)

Back when I was applying for colleges, Michigan Tech was one of the top contender. Because of this, I applied for a few scholarships that they had. One of them was the 'Leading Scholar Award', where I had to write an essay that revolved my leadership related experience. I ended up writting an essay about how I use internet memes to connect with people and make them laugh, and was a winner! My mom and I flew to Houghton, Michigan at first, for what wa supposed to be a weekend event and tour at Michigan Tech. However, there was a terrible blizzard that kept us stuck in Houghton for an extra three days. In the end, my mom and I decided to rent a car and drive the eight hours from Houghton to Grand Rapids. It was very beautiful driving through the upper peninsula and Mackinaw Bridge in the winter!

Washington DC and Massanutten, Virginia
(August 15-25, 2018)

After I finished my first cycling trip to Mackinaw with my dad, my family went on vacation together. My parents had wanted to travel to Washington DC for some time, since they're foreigners and wanted to see the capital. They also found several interesting things to do in Virginia, like Massanutten Resort, Luray Caverns, and Monticello. In the end, we had a road trip traveling to each of these locations. It was fun!

Vacations

My parents have always held a very high value for traveling. They must've had the dream to travel and see the world. Whatever the case, we travel relatively frequently for vacations. Here are some of them.

Japan
(July 7-26, 2024)

My family had been planning on traveling to Japan for years before we finally went. My mom wanted to go back in 2020, but then the pandemic happened, and we put the plan on the sideline for a while. After I graduated highschool, I put the idea up in the air again, and we all agreed that it was the perfect time to go. I had just graduated, so it was like a reward for me. I had also been learning Japanese in my free time as a hobby, so I was trained enough to help out if needed. Additionaly, the economy at the time had a good dollar to yen conversion rate. We traveled to all different types of temples, museums, theme parks, restaurants, and parks. I designed and scheduled the entire itinerary, and my family agreed that it was the best vacation that we had ever had!

Mediterranean Cruise
(July 7-22, 2023)

My family had considered taking a cruise for years, and we finally decided to take one around the mediterranean area. The cruise we took traveled from Italy to Greece, Turkey and back. Notable places we visited were Vennice, and Mykonos. I believe Mykonos in particular was the most beautiful place that I had ever seen. It was fun!
